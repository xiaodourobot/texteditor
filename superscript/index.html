What Is Superscript Text?

The easiest way to explain what superscript text is that superscript visually is distorted as such, for example; it is a small letter/number above a particular letter/number such as - 25. The number 5 above the number 2 is an example of superscript.

Using superscript within social media/messaging/documents is basically to draw attention; it’s not used for its original purpose.

Fun-fact:

Did you know that superscript was designed and created within mathematics and science?

Superscript has many uses within math and science, most commonly is to show an exponent (i.e., duplicated multiplication of a number by itself, such as squaring or cubing a number). This is also known as a “power” number: So “four cubed” is written in superscript text as 43.
